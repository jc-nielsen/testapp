Wireframes are not final designs to code against.


Wireframes are intended to show key flows and user interactions while being flexible enough for rapid prototyping. They are often only partially interactive, lack realistic data, and use placeholder color choices, fonts, and iconography. They are used for sharing design patterns, usability testing, and requirements validation.  In other words, they do not show final visual design choices  (colors, spacing, icons, fonts, animations etc.) that should be used for coding a project. Please refer to our visual design standards and comps for exact UI guidance.


NOTE: Visual comps are created after iterating on wireframes with product leadership, user testing, and UX design. If you need final visual design comps, please reach out to the UX team that is working on this project.
